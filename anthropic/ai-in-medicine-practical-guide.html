Part 1: The Case Presentation

We begin with a realistic clinical scenario: a 68-year-old patient presenting with cognitive decline. This case demonstrates how AI can help clinicians process complex patient information more efficiently. We'll explore the brain regions involved and see how technology can transform raw clinical notes into structured, actionable summaries.

A 68-year-old male presents with a 3-month history of progressive word-finding difficulties and intermittent confusion. His family reports recent episodes of getting lost in familiar places and difficulty managing his finances. His past medical history is significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia, both well-controlled on medication. He has no history of stroke or traumatic brain injury.

Part 2: How LLM Technology Can Assist

Large Language Models (LLMs) are powerful tools that can parse, summarize, and structure vast amounts of text. In a clinical setting, this can significantly reduce administrative burden and highlight key information from lengthy patient notes. An LLM can take a narrative HPI (History of Present Illness) and structure it into a concise, organized summary, making it easier for consulting physicians to quickly grasp the case.

LLM-Enhanced Note

Patient: 68-Year-Old Male

Chief Complaint: Cognitive Decline

History of Present Illness: Patient presents with a 3-month history of progressive cognitive decline, characterized by:

  • Anomic aphasia (word-finding difficulty).
  • Visuospatial disorientation (getting lost in familiar locations).
  • Executive dysfunction (difficulty with financial management).

Past Medical History: Hypertension, Hyperlipidemia (well-controlled).

Review of Systems: No history of CVA or TBI. Neurological exam within normal limits except for cognitive deficits noted above.

Original Raw Note

pt is a 68yo man here w/ family. they say he's been having trouble finding his words for maybe 3 mo. also getting confused. wife says he got lost driving home from the grocery store last week, a trip he's made for 20 yrs. can't handle the bills anymore. hx of htn and high chol, but takes meds for it. denies any weakness or numbness. no head trauma. i checked him out, neuro exam is pretty much normal, cn ii-xii intact, 5/5 strength throughout. but his mmse score was low and he couldn't draw the clock right. seems like something's going on with his thinking. maybe alzheimers? need to investigate further.

Part 3: The Clinician's AI Workbench

So, how do we get from the raw note to the structured summary? This is done using an interface often called a "Workbench" or "Console." Instead of a static image, the diagram below is fully interactive. Hover over key elements like the sidebar buttons or the "Run" button to learn what they do.

Deconstructing the Workbench

To use this tool effectively, you don't need to be a programmer; you just need to be good at giving clear instructions. Think of it like delegating a task to a junior resident. Let's break down the key components you see in the interactive diagram above.

  • System Prompt: This is where you set the AI's permanent role or personality. For clinical work, you might set it to: "You are a helpful medical scribe. Your task is to take raw clinical notes and reformat them into a clear, structured HPI using standard medical terminology. Be concise and accurate." This instruction acts as a permanent filter, ensuring every note you process adheres to the same professional standard.
  • User Prompt: This is the main instruction box for your specific, one-time task. Here, you would paste the raw, dictated note from our 68-year-old male patient and add a simple command like, "Please structure the following note:" followed by the text. This is the "what to do right now" part of the instruction.
  • Run Button: This is the "Go" button. Clicking it executes your command. The AI takes the text from the User Prompt and processes it according to the rules you established in the System Prompt.
  • Output Panel (Right Side): This is where the clean, structured, and ready-to-use clinical note appears. From here, it can be reviewed for accuracy and then copied directly into the patient's Electronic Health Record (EHR), saving significant time.
  • Sidebar Navigation (Left Side): This area helps you organize your work. The Workbench is your main workspace. The Dashboard provides an overview of your activity, while Files allows you to save prompts or upload documents for the AI to analyze, such as a patient's past medical records.
